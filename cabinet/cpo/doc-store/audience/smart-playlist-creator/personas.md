# 🎭 Audience Personas: YouTube Smart Playlist Creator

**Date**: May 31, 2026
**Reviewer**: Customer/User Skill

## 👤 Persona 1: The Pragmatic Music Fan

**Tag:** `audience:pragmatic-music-fan`

**Name:** Priya, 28
**Tagline:** "I just want to hear good music without spending 20 minutes building a playlist."

**Demographics:**
- Occupation: Software engineer
- Location: Bangalore, India
- Tech literacy: High
- Devices: MacBook Pro, iPhone 14, good headphones

**Psychographics:**
- Primary goal: Find new music in her preferred genres with zero friction
- Values most: Speed, relevance, auto-play
- Hates: Slow load times, irrelevant results, having to create YouTube playlists manually
- Decision style: Impulsive — if it doesn't work in 10 seconds, she moves on

**Daily Context:**
- Uses during work hours (background music) and commute
- Always has multiple tabs open, short attention span
- Internet: Fast fiber at home, 5G on mobile

**Competing Alternatives:**
- Spotify (but wants YouTube content — lofi, lectures, rare tracks)
- YouTube's built-in mix feature (but limited filtering)
- Manually searching and adding to playlists (current behavior)

**Frustrations with current solutions:**
1. YouTube mixes are random, not curated to her taste
2. Spotify doesn't have the long-tail YouTube content she wants
3. Building playlists manually is tedious and time-consuming

## 👤 Persona 3: The Power Curator

**Tag:** `audience:power-curator`

**Name:** Maya, 32
**Tagline:** "I manage 20+ playlists for every mood. Give me power tools."

**Demographics:**
- Occupation: Content creator / DJ
- Location: Berlin, Germany
- Tech literacy: High
- Devices: Custom PC, iPad Pro, iPhone 15, studio monitors

**Psychographics:**
- Primary goal: Create themed, perfectly filtered playlists for different contexts
- Values most: Granular filters, precision, batch operations, export options
- Hates: Limited options, arbitrary restrictions, no keyboard shortcuts
- Decision style: Methodical — optimizes every detail

**Daily Context:**
- Creates playlists for gym, study, parties, and client work
- Multiple monitors, often has 20+ browser tabs open
- Internet: High-speed fiber

**Competing Alternatives:**
- Currently uses Airtable + manual YouTube bookmarks
- Soundiiz for playlist management
- Spreadsheet of YouTube video IDs

**Frustrations with current solutions:**
1. No tool combines search + filters + auto-play
2. Manual curation is incredibly time-consuming
3. YouTube's API limits creative playlist building
